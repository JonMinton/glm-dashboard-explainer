The Beta Distribution

The Beta distribution is parameterised by two positive shape parameters $a$ and $b$. It is the natural distribution for continuous random variables on the open interval $(0, 1)$.

Probability Density Function

$$f(y; a, b) = \frac{y^{a-1}(1-y)^{b-1}}{B(a, b)}, \quad y \in (0, 1)$$

Where $B(a, b) = \frac{\Gamma(a)\Gamma(b)}{\Gamma(a+b)}$ is the Beta function.

GLM Parameterisation

For regression, we reparameterise in terms of the mean $\mu$ and precision $\phi$:

  • $a = \mu \phi$,   $b = (1 - \mu) \phi$
  • Mean: $E[Y] = \mu = a/(a+b)$
  • Precision: $\phi = a + b$
  • Variance: $\text{Var}(Y) = \mu(1-\mu)/(1 + \phi)$

GLM Form of the PDF

Substituting $a = \mu\phi$ and $b = (1-\mu)\phi$:

$$f(y; \mu, \phi) = \frac{\Gamma(\phi)}{\Gamma(\mu\phi)\,\Gamma((1-\mu)\phi)} \; y^{\mu\phi - 1} (1-y)^{(1-\mu)\phi - 1}$$

Deriving the Log-Likelihood

Step 1: Log-likelihood for one observation
$$\ell_i = \ln\Gamma(\phi) - \ln\Gamma(\mu_i\phi) - \ln\Gamma((1-\mu_i)\phi) + (\mu_i\phi - 1)\ln y_i + ((1-\mu_i)\phi - 1)\ln(1-y_i)$$
Step 2: Sum over all observations
$$\ell(\boldsymbol{\beta}, \phi) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \Big[ \ln\Gamma(\phi) - \ln\Gamma(\mu_i\phi) - \ln\Gamma((1-\mu_i)\phi) + (\mu_i\phi - 1)\ln y_i + ((1-\mu_i)\phi - 1)\ln(1-y_i) \Big]$$
Step 3: With logit link, $\mu_i = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-X_i'\beta}}$

The mean $\mu_i$ enters through the logit link. Derivatives with respect to $\beta$ use the chain rule through the logistic function.

Key Observation: Two Sets of Parameters

Unlike standard GLMs where dispersion is often a nuisance parameter, Beta regression estimates both $\beta$ (mean model) and $\phi$ (precision) via MLE simultaneously.

This is why Beta regression uses direct numerical optimisation rather than IRLS.

The Score Function

Taking the derivative of $\ell$ with respect to $\beta$ requires the chain rule through the logit link:

$$\frac{\partial \ell}{\partial \beta} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{\partial \ell_i}{\partial \mu_i} \cdot \frac{\partial \mu_i}{\partial \eta_i} \cdot X_i$$

Where $\frac{\partial \mu_i}{\partial \eta_i} = \mu_i(1-\mu_i)$ and:

$$\frac{\partial \ell_i}{\partial \mu_i} = \phi \Big[ \ln y_i - \ln(1-y_i) - \psi(\mu_i\phi) + \psi((1-\mu_i)\phi) \Big]$$

Here $\psi(\cdot)$ is the digamma function — the derivative of $\ln\Gamma(\cdot)$.

Why IRLS Doesn't Work Directly

The digamma functions make the score more complex than standard GLMs. Beta regression uses Newton-Raphson or Fisher scoring rather than IRLS.

Understanding the Precision Parameter

$$\text{Var}(Y) = \frac{\mu(1-\mu)}{1+\phi}$$

For Our Exam Proportions Model

Precision: $\hat{\phi} \approx 30.5$

For a predicted proportion of $\mu = 0.7$: SD $= \sqrt{0.7 \times 0.3 / 31.5} \approx 0.082$ (about 8 percentage points).

Unlike Gaussian, the variance automatically decreases near the boundaries (0 and 1).

Key takeaways: logit link for (0,1) constraint, Beta distribution for continuous proportions, and precision parameter $\phi$ controlling dispersion.
